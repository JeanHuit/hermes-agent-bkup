# Review Criteria Specification

A submission must clear an acceptable bar on all three dimensions below to pass — a strong score in one dimension does not offset a weak score in another.

## Required Input Materials (checked at Intake, Stage I)

1. **Abstract/Concept Summary** — max ~500 words covering the problem, proposed solution, and novelty claim.
2. **Methodology Deep Dive** — how the claims will be tested: data sources, experimental controls, necessary tools, resource estimates.
3. **Literature Review / State-of-the-Art** — minimum 5 relevant citations linking the current knowledge gap to the proposed solution.
4. **Executive Summary + high-level Technical Diagram** (also required at intake per the Council SOP).

## I. Scientific and Technical Rigor — "Does it work?"
Robustness, reliability, and scientific merit of the underlying principles.

1. **Reproducibility** — methodology described with enough detail that a competent third party could replicate the core experiments/analyses; plans to mitigate systematic bias are present.
2. **Scientific Novelty** — a clear, defensible leap beyond current established methods, not mere incremental improvement, while still building on cited prior work.
3. **Core Scientific Merit** — the problem is well-defined against known scientific principles, with clear awareness of both successes and limitations in the field.

## II. Implementation Feasibility — "Can we build it?"
Tractability, resource availability, developmental maturity.

1. **Technology Readiness Level (TRL)** — core technology components assessed against a TRL-style framework; flag high risk if the solution depends on multiple foundational technologies that aren't yet mature or combined in any known working prototype. Must show a clear path to a Minimum Viable Product (MVP).
2. **Resource and Time Estimate** — a documented high-level project plan: key milestones, personnel needs, required capital/infrastructure.
3. **Domain Expertise** — completeness of the team's expertise relative to the problem space; any gaps need a clear hiring or collaboration strategy.

## III. Market and System Potential — "Is it valuable?"
Commercial viability, user adoption, systemic impact.

1. **Problem-Value Fit** — evidence of an acute, unmet need in a defined target market segment; the solution addresses a pain point that costs the user real time, money, or effort.
2. **Economic Viability (Business Model)** — analysis via standard frameworks (e.g. competitive-forces analysis, minimum acceptable ROI) proving commercial scalability and defensibility.
3. **User Adoption Curve** — a rollout strategy that accounts for how the solution moves through the technology adoption lifecycle (innovators → early adopters → majority).

## Mapping criteria to council roles

- **Technical Specialist** primarily evaluates Section I (Scientific/Technical Rigor) and the TRL/feasibility items in Section II.
- **Domain Application Expert** primarily evaluates Section III (Market and System Potential) and the resourcing/team-fit items in Section II.
- **Ethical & Impact Reviewer** evaluates ethical alignment and regulatory/societal risk across all three sections — this role isn't confined to a single criteria section and should flag concerns anywhere they appear (e.g. a technically sound but ethically risky data-collection method, or a commercially attractive but exploitative adoption strategy).

Each individual review (Stage II) should cite the specific numbered benchmark(s) above that support its Pass/Fail vote, rather than giving an unanchored opinion.
